EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India's national nickel strategic reserve stands at 45,200 MT of nickel equivalent (Ni-eq) as of 
31 March 2025, representing approximately 5.8 months of projected demand based on NCMM Demand 
Model v2.3 (February 2025). The NCMM FY2025-26 target is 60,000 MT by 31 December 2025, requiring 
an additional 14,800 MT to be procured in the remaining three quarters of FY2025-26. Nickel inventory 
is distributed across three primary storage facilities: Visakhapatnam Port Strategic Depot (28,000 MT), 
JNPT Strategic Warehouse JNPT-W08 (17,000 MT), and Nellore Strategic Reserve Depot, APIIC Industrial 
Area (200 MT). An additional allocation of 3,200 MT from the Q1 MV EASTERN PROMISE consignment 
(Manifest Ref: VIZAG/PORT-OPS/NKL/Q1/2025/0071) is pending warehouse assignment and will be 
incorporated in the Q2 inventory report. This document provides a detailed grade-wise breakdown and 
strategic reserve gap analysis.

SECTION 6: RECOMMENDATIONS

6.1 URGENT: Issue KABIL procurement directive to accelerate Q2 imports — recommend emergency 
    tender for additional 6,000 MT Class I nickel from Philippines (alternate Coral Bay supplier) 
    and Indonesia (Harita Nickel) to close the gap before 31 December 2025 target date.

6.2 Expand Nellore depot to 3,000 MT capacity on priority — Civil works to be awarded by May 2025.

6.3 Commission NiSO₄ processing unit at KABIL Vadodara — to convert Class I cathode to battery-grade 
    nickel sulfate in-house (INR 380 Crore capex; MECON DPR submitted January 2025).

6.4 Establish MoU with RINL (Rashtriya Ispat Nigam Ltd.) for ferronickel secondary reserve program — 
    RINL's Vizag Steel Plant consumes 12,000 MT Ni/year; potential swap-reserve agreement feasible.
