EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
India's EV battery sector will require exponentially growing quantities of critical 
minerals through 2035. This forecast quantifies demand by mineral and year, identifies 
strategic reserve gaps, and estimates investment requirements to achieve supply security.

INDIA EV MARKET PROJECTION:
Based on SIAM data and FAME-III targets:
- FY2025: 2.1 million EV units (4W+2W+3W combined)
- FY2027: 5.8 million EV units  
- FY2030: 14.2 million EV units
- FY2035: 28.5 million EV units (EV penetration ~40% of new vehicle sales)

MINERAL DEMAND BY YEAR (METRIC TONNES):

LITHIUM (as LCE):
FY2025: 38,400 MT | FY2027: 105,900 MT | FY2030: 258,900 MT | FY2035: 519,100 MT
Current strategic reserve: 18,750 MT (0.7 months FY2030 demand)
TARGET: Build to 24-month coverage of projected demand at all times

COBALT:
FY2025: 3,840 MT | FY2027: 10,590 MT | FY2030: 25,890 MT | FY2035: 51,910 MT
(Assumes 50% NMC, 30% LFP by FY2030 — cobalt intensity declines)
Current strategic reserve: 8,400 MT (3.9 months FY2030 demand)

NICKEL (battery grade):
FY2025: 19,200 MT | FY2027: 52,960 MT | FY2030: 129,460 MT | FY2035: 259,560 MT
Current strategic reserve: 42,600 MT (3.9 months FY2030 demand)

MANGANESE (high-purity):
FY2025: 7,680 MT | FY2027: 21,180 MT | FY2030: 51,780 MT | FY2035: 103,820 MT

NATURAL GRAPHITE (spherical, anode):
FY2025: 76,800 MT | FY2027: 211,800 MT | FY2030: 517,800 MT | FY2035: 1,038,200 MT
CRITICAL GAP: Current reserve 2,400 MT (< 1 week FY2030 demand). HIGHEST PRIORITY MINERAL.

RARE EARTH ELEMENTS (NdPr oxide for EV motors):
FY2025: 2,400 MT | FY2027: 6,620 MT | FY2030: 16,180 MT | FY2035: 32,450 MT

INVESTMENT REQUIREMENTS (to achieve 24-month reserve coverage by FY2030):
- Lithium procurement: INR 48,200 crore
- Cobalt procurement: INR 18,900 crore
- Nickel procurement: INR 52,400 crore
- Graphite procurement: INR 12,800 crore (MOST URGENT)
- REE procurement: INR 18,600 crore
- TOTAL: INR 1,50,900 crore (USD 18.1 billion) over FY2025-30

RISK SCENARIO ANALYSIS:
Scenario A (Base): India achieves FY2030 EV targets with 24-month reserve coverage
Scenario B (Disruption): China restricts graphite exports in FY2026 — India's EV 
   manufacturing halts within 30 days without emergency stocks
Scenario C (Price Shock): Lithium spot price doubles — LCE budget shortfall INR 18,000 crore

CRITICAL ALERT — GRAPHITE GAP:
Graphite is the single most critical immediate vulnerability. India has near-zero strategic 
reserves (2,400 MT vs. 76,800 MT FY2025 annual demand). Emergency procurement of 
12-month graphite anode stock (76,800 MT) is recommended as Priority 1 action.
