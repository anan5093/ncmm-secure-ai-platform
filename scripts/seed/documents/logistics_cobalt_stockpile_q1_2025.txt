EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India's cobalt strategic reserve as of 31 March 2025 stands at 4,780 Metric Tonnes (MT) of cobalt 
equivalent (Co-eq), representing approximately 6.2 months of projected industrial demand at current 
consumption rates. This is marginally below the NCMM Phase-1 target of 7,500 MT (12-month coverage) 
stipulated under the National Critical Mineral Mission Framework Document (MoM/NCMM/2023/FW-001) 
issued on 29 September 2023. The shortfall of 2,720 MT remains a significant concern given accelerating 
EV adoption and battery manufacturing expansion under the PLI-ACC (Production Linked Incentive — 
Advanced Chemistry Cell) scheme. DRC-origin cobalt continues to dominate India's import basket at 67% 
of total procurement, creating a concentrated geopolitical exposure that this report recommends addressing 
urgently through supplier diversification.

CRITICAL OBSERVATION: DRC DEPENDENCY — 67% (HIGH RISK)

The DRC accounts for approximately 70% of global cobalt production (per USGS Mineral Commodity 
Summaries 2025). Any political instability in Katanga Province (conflict minerals zone) or changes 
to DRC's mineral export tax regime (currently under review by DRC parliament, Bill MINES-DRC-2024-78) 
could disrupt India's cobalt supply within 45-90 days. The M23 rebel activity in eastern DRC and 
Rwandan border tensions as of Q1 2025 present a near-term supply disruption risk rated at Level 3 
(Elevated) on the NCMM Supply Risk Matrix.

SECTION 4: DEPLETION RATE & COVERAGE ANALYSIS

Monthly consumption rate (FY2025-26 projected): 771 MT Co-eq/month
Current stock: 4,780 MT
Coverage ratio: 4,780 ÷ 771 = 6.2 months

NCMM Phase-1 Target: 12 months coverage = 9,252 MT required
Current gap: 9,252 - 4,780 = 4,472 MT shortfall (48.3% below target)

Consumption Projection (NCMM Demand Model v2.3, Feb 2025):
  FY2025-26: 9,250 MT/year (771 MT/month)
  FY2026-27: 13,800 MT/year (accelerated by Maruti Suzuki ACC facility, Haryana + Mahindra EV line)
  FY2027-28: 18,400 MT/year (full ramp-up of PLI-ACC Tranche-1 and Tranche-2 facilities)
  FY2028-29: 24,100 MT/year (projected if domestic battery manufacturing achieves 50 GWh/year target)

At projected FY2026-27 demand, current stockpile covers only 4.2 months — a critical vulnerability.

SECTION 6: RECOMMENDATIONS

6.1 IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (0–3 months)
  (a) Accelerate procurement from Australia and Zambia to reduce DRC share to ≤55% by Q2 FY2025-26.
  (b) Activate stand-by supply agreement with Freeport Cobalt (Finland) for emergency 1,000 MT supply
      at agreed ceiling price of USD 28,000/MT — Agreement ref: KABIL/FIN/COBALT/2024/SA-003.
  (c) Expedite customs clearance of 890 MT presently held at KOPT — pending AERB clearance since
      15 February 2025; follow-up required with AERB Regional Director (Eastern), Kolkata.

6.2 MEDIUM-TERM ACTIONS (3–12 months)
  (a) Negotiate G2G cobalt supply agreement with Government of Zambia (proposed MoU under 
      India-Africa Critical Minerals Partnership, announced at IAMD Nairobi Summit, Jan 2025).
  (b) Commission cobalt recovery facility at NMDC's Singhbhum copper project — target: 150 MT/year
      domestic cobalt by FY2027-28 (see companion report: NCMM/GEO/COB/JHK/2025/REP-012).
  (c) Establish cobalt exchange recycling programme targeting EoL batteries — estimated recovery 
      potential: 200–400 MT/year by FY2027-28 (CPCB Battery Waste Management Rules 2022 framework).

6.3 POLICY RECOMMENDATIONS
  (a) Declare cobalt as a "Hyper-Critical Mineral" under updated NCMM classification 
      (current classification: Critical — Category A).
  (b) Enhance strategic reserve target to 15-month coverage by end of FY2026-27.
  (c) Introduce cobalt hedging mechanism through Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) — 
      proposal under review with SEBI and Ministry of Finance.
