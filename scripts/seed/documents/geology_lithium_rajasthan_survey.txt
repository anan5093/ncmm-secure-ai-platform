EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
The Geological Survey of India has completed Phase II exploration of the Degana-Nagaur 
lithium deposit. Updated inferred resource estimate: 5.9 million tonnes of lithium 
ore grading 0.18% Li (lithium oxide equivalent: 1,900 ppm Li). This represents 
India's largest known lithium deposit and has strategic significance for domestic supply.

GEOLOGY AND MINERALOGY:
The lithium mineralisation occurs in a Precambrian granite-pegmatite complex intruded 
into Aravalli metasedimentary sequences. Primary lithium mineral: Lepidolite (K(Li,Al)3(Al,Si,Rb)4O10(F,OH)2) 
with secondary occurrences of Zinnwaldite and Lithian muscovite.

Peraluminous two-mica granites host the mineralisation across a 14.2 km strike length 
and 1.8 km width. Depth of mineralisation confirmed by diamond drilling to 280 metres 
below surface.

RESOURCE ESTIMATION (JORC 2012 Compliant):
- Inferred Resource: 5.9 million tonnes (Mt) @ 0.18% Li
- Li contained metal (inferred): 10,620 tonnes Li
- Lithium Carbonate Equivalent (LCE): 56,400 tonnes LCE
- Confidence Level: INFERRED (requires infill drilling for Indicated/Measured upgrade)

DRILLING RESULTS SUMMARY:
Phase I (2022-23): 12 drill holes, 1,840m total. Best intercept: 42.3m @ 0.24% Li
Phase II (2023-24): 28 drill holes, 5,260m total. Best intercept: 67.8m @ 0.31% Li
Confirmatory hole DEGNAG-24-28: 0.28% Li over 52.1m (significant high-grade zone identified)

PROCESSING CONSIDERATIONS:
Testwork indicates the ore is amenable to flotation concentration followed by 
sulphation roasting or pressure leaching. Estimated lithium recovery: 72-78%.
Water requirement: 4.2 m³/tonne ore (requires pipeline from Luni River, 28 km).
Environmental sensitivity: Rajasthan desert ecosystem — EIA mandatory.

DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE:
- FY2025-26: Feasibility study and detailed project report (DPR)
- FY2026-27: Environmental Impact Assessment and public consultations
- FY2027-28: Mining lease application and project approval
- FY2029-30: Earliest commercial production (estimate: 2,000 MT LCE/annum)
- Peak production: 8,000 MT LCE/annum by FY2032-33

STRATEGIC VALUE ASSESSMENT:
At peak production, the Degana-Nagaur deposit can supply approximately 32% of India's 
projected domestic lithium demand in FY2032. This materially reduces import dependency.
At current lithium prices (INR 1,850/kg LCE), the deposit's gross resource value 
is approximately INR 1,04,340 crore (USD 12.5 billion).
