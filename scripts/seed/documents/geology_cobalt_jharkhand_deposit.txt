EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
Phase I reconnaissance survey of the Singhbhum Copper Belt has identified significant 
cobalt mineralisation as a by-product of copper sulphide ores. Inferred cobalt resource: 
2.1 million tonnes of ore grading 0.048% Co, with 1,008 tonnes of contained cobalt metal.

COBALT MINERALISATION:
Cobalt occurs as cobaltite (CoAsS), safflorite (CoAs2), and as a minor constituent in 
chalcopyrite. The Singhbhum belt hosts India's most significant known cobalt occurrence.
Hindustan Copper Limited (HCL) currently mines the belt for copper; cobalt recovery 
has not previously been attempted due to low economic priority.

RESOURCE ESTIMATE:
- Total Ore (Inferred): 2.1 Mt @ 0.048% Co average grade
- Contained Cobalt Metal: ~1,008 tonnes
- Associated Copper: 0.98% Cu (economic by-product)
- Confidence: INFERRED — requires systematic infill drilling

DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE:
- FY2025-26: Detailed resource delineation drilling program (60 drill holes, 12,000m)
- FY2027-28: Pre-feasibility study for cobalt recovery circuit addition to HCL's existing plants
- FY2029-30: Pilot plant commissioning
- FY2031-32: Commercial production (estimated: 80-100 tonnes Co/annum)

LIMITATIONS:
At 80-100 tonnes Co/annum, domestic production covers only 3.5% of India's 
projected cobalt demand in FY2032. Domestic geology does not support primary 
cobalt supply independence — import dependency will remain structural.

RECOMMENDATION: Classify as supplementary domestic source while continuing 
aggressive international procurement strategy per NCMM-POLICY-FY26-001.
