RARE EARTH ELEMENT (REE) PROCUREMENT PLAN
FY2025-26 TO FY2027-28
REDUCING CHINA DEPENDENCY — STRATEGIC DIVERSIFICATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India's rare earth element (REE) supply chain is critically dependent on China, which currently 
accounts for 82% of India's rare earth imports across all 17 rare earth elements (including both 
light rare earth elements — LREE — and heavy rare earth elements — HREE). This dependency represents 
India's single greatest critical mineral vulnerability given China's demonstrated willingness to 
weaponize mineral supply chains (export restrictions on gallium and germanium, August 2023; 
antimony restrictions, September 2024). This procurement plan outlines a phased strategy to reduce 
China's share to below 35% by FY2027-28 through diversified sourcing from Australia, Canada, Brazil, 
and South Africa, supported by enhanced domestic processing through Indian Rare Earths Limited (IREL). 
Total estimated expenditure for this plan: INR 4,200 Crore over three fiscal years.

Critical Observation: India's 82% dependency on Chinese REE is among the highest in the world. 
Even "Australian" supply from Lynas Rare Earths involves Chinese-developed processing chemistry. 
India has no domestic rare earth separation or refining capacity of scale outside of IREL's limited 
monazite processing at Odisha and Tamil Nadu coastal separation plants (combined capacity: ~800 MT 
TREO/year, using legacy monazite beach-sand feedstock).

SECTION 4: COUNTRY-WISE PROCUREMENT STRATEGY

4.1 AUSTRALIA — Target: 37.5% share by FY2027-28

Supplier A: Lynas Rare Earths Limited (ASX: LYC)
  - Current supply: 1,204 MT TREO/year (mixed oxides — La, Ce, Nd, Pr, Sm)
  - Source: Mount Weld, WA (mine) → Malaysia processing (Kuantan LAMP facility)
  - India-dedicated stream: KABIL-Lynas Supply Agreement (Ref: KABIL/AUS/REE/2024/001, 
    signed 08 August 2024 during PM Modi-Albanese bilateral)
  - Target FY2025-26: 3,200 MT TREO
  - Price: USD 12.40/kg TREO (NdPr oxide component priced separately at USD 52/kg NdPr)
  - New development: Lynas Texas Processing Facility (US DOD funded) — India negotiating 
    co-access arrangement through QUAD Minerals Partnership (QMP)

Supplier B: Arafura Rare Earths (ASX: ARU)
  - Project: Nolans Bore, Northern Territory (fully integrated mine to NdPr oxide)
  - Production start: FY2026-27 (target 9,000 MT NdPr oxide/year)
  - India off-take: LOI signed November 2024 for 2,000 MT NdPr/year for 10 years
  - KABIL equity consideration: 15% stake proposed (Cabinet Note under preparation, March 2025)

Supplier C: Meteoric Resources (HREE focus)
  - Project: Caldeira, Minas Gerais, Brazil (ion adsorption clay — HREE rich)
  - Note: Though Brazilian-based, Australian-listed; included in Australia strategy
  - Target: 480 MT HREE oxides (Dy, Tb, Gd, Y) by FY2026-27

4.2 CANADA — Target: 10% share by FY2027-28

Supplier: Vital Metals Limited (ASX: VML)
  - Project: Nechalacho, Northwest Territories
  - Production: Light REE concentrate (LREE)
  - India talks: Exploratory discussions at Canada-India Critical Minerals Summit, Ottawa, 
    September 2024
  - Target: 1,920 MT TREO by FY2027-28
  - Risk: Vital Metals processing at Saskatoon facility scale-up uncertain

Canada G2G Framework: India-Canada Critical Minerals Working Group (CMWG) established under 
India-Canada Strategic Partnership — currently on pause pending resolution of diplomatic tensions 
(Nijjar affair residual issues). MEA advises cautious engagement with commercial pathways only 
until governmental relations normalise (target: H2 FY2025-26).

4.3 BRAZIL — Target: 2.5% share (HREE focus)

Supplier: Serra Verde Mining (ion adsorption clay HREE)
  - Location: Minaçu, Goiás state
  - Products: Dy₂O₃, Tb₄O₇, Y₂O₃, Gd₂O₃
  - India off-take agreement: Under negotiation (KABIL Brazil desk, target Q3 FY2025-26)
  
4.4 INDIA (IREL Domestic) — Target: 15% share by FY2027-28

Current IREL Operations:
  - Beach Sand Mineral processing: Chavara (Kerala), Manavalakurichi (Tamil Nadu), 
    Chhatrapur (Odisha)
  - Current TREO production: ~536 MT/year (monazite-derived)
  - Products: Mixed RECO₃ (rare earth carbonate), low-purity oxides
  - Constraint: Monazite (thorium-bearing; BARC/DAE regulatory framework applies)

IREL Expansion Plan (MoM Approved, Feb 2025):
  - New IREL Separation Plant at Visakhapatnam SEZ: 3,000 MT TREO/year capacity
  - Technology partner: Shortlisted — Solvay (Belgium) or Less Common Metals (UK)
  - Capex: INR 1,480 Crore (MoM Budget allocation FY2025-26: INR 410 Crore initial)
  - Target commissioning: FY2028-29

SECTION 6: RISK MITIGATION — CHINA EXPOSURE

Primary risk: China could implement REE export restrictions against India (precedent: 2010 
Japan embargo, 2023 gallium/germanium restrictions). Specific concern: HREE (Dy, Tb) where 
China controls ~97% of global production; no viable alternative sourcing at scale before FY2028.

Mitigation: Accelerate HREE strategic buffer — target 24-month HREE reserve by FY2026-27.
           Estimated cost: INR 890 Crore for 350 MT HREE oxides at current prices.
