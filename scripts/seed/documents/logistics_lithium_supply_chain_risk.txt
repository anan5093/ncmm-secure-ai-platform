INTELLIGENCE REPORT — SUPPLY CHAIN RISK ASSESSMENT
LITHIUM: INDIA'S STRATEGIC VULNERABILITY ANALYSIS
FY2025-26
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India's lithium supply chain is in a state of acute vulnerability as of Q1 FY2025-26. The country 
currently holds only 8 months of strategic lithium reserve coverage against projected demand for 
FY2025-26, falling dangerously short of the NCMM-mandated 18-month buffer target. Three compounding 
factors drive this risk: (1) Australian export policy uncertainty following the Critical Minerals 
Strategic Review (CMSR) tabled in Canberra in January 2025; (2) extreme lithium price volatility — 
LCE (Lithium Carbonate Equivalent) prices declined 68% between January 2023 and March 2025, creating 
commercial procurement complexity; and (3) India's near-zero domestic lithium production, despite the 
GSI-confirmed Degana-Nagaur discovery in Rajasthan (see companion document: NCMM/GEO/LIT/RAJ/2025/
GS-007). This report provides a structured risk assessment and recommends a multi-pronged mitigation 
strategy incorporating Argentina, Chile, and domestic production pathways.

SECTION 3: AUSTRALIA SUPPLY RISK — CRITICAL ANALYSIS

Australia supplies approximately 55% of India's lithium imports (primarily as spodumene concentrate, 
SC6.0 grade) and is the world's largest lithium producer (89,000 MT LCE production in 2024 per 
Geoscience Australia). However, multiple risk factors have emerged:

3.1 Australian Export Policy Review (CMSR 2025)
  The Australian government's Critical Minerals Strategic Review (tabled February 2025) proposes:
  - Mandatory "value-add" processing before export of certain critical mineral concentrates by FY2027
  - Possible export controls on lithium concentrates to "non-allied" nations under AUKUS framework
  - Priority allocation mechanisms for QUAD partners and Five Eyes nations
  
  NCMM Assessment: If Australia implements processing-before-export mandate, India's spodumene 
  concentrate imports will cease by mid-FY2026-27, forcing procurement to shift to processed forms 
  (lithium carbonate/hydroxide) at 2–3x higher unit cost. Estimated additional annual cost: 
  INR 8,400 crore at current demand levels.

3.2 Key Australian Suppliers at Risk
  - Pilbara Minerals (Pilgangoora, WA): 280,000 MT SC6.0/year capacity; India off-take: 45,000 MT/yr
  - Liontown Resources (Kathleen Valley, WA): First production 2024; India off-take: 20,000 MT/yr
  - Core Lithium (Finniss, NT): Under care-and-maintenance as of Feb 2025 (price-driven suspension)

3.3 Australia Risk Rating: HIGH (Score: 7.8/10 on NCMM Supply Risk Matrix)

SECTION 4: PRICE VOLATILITY ANALYSIS

Lithium price history (LCE, China spot, CNY/MT equivalent in USD):
  Jan 2023: USD 78,000/MT (peak)
  Jul 2023: USD 42,000/MT
  Jan 2024: USD 15,800/MT
  Jul 2024: USD 12,100/MT
  Jan 2025: USD 10,200/MT
  Mar 2025: USD 9,850/MT (current)

Price decline driver analysis:
  (a) Chinese lepidolite production surge (low-cost brine processing)
  (b) CATL and BYD sodium-ion battery introduction reducing Li demand signals
  (c) Global EV demand slowdown in EU and US markets (subsidy phase-outs)
  (d) Argentina and Chile capacity additions (Lithium Americas, SQM expansions)

Impact on NCMM procurement:
  Low prices create a window of opportunity for large-scale forward procurement and 
  reserve-building at lowest cost since 2021. NCMM Finance Division recommends accelerating 
  procurement while LCE remains below USD 12,000/MT. Estimated savings: INR 2,200 crore 
  versus FY2023 procurement costs.

SECTION 5: ALTERNATIVE SOURCING — ARGENTINA & CHILE

5.1 Argentina (Lithium Triangle — Salta, Jujuy, Catamarca provinces)
  - Production capacity: 45,000 MT LCE/year (2025); projected 120,000 MT LCE/year by 2027
  - Key producers: POSCO Holdings (Hombre Muerto), Allkem-Livent (Fenix/Olaroz), Zijin Mining
  - India engagement: KABIL-Argentina MOU signed 12 February 2025 (on margins of G20 Finance 
    Ministers Meeting, Cape Town) — covers 10,000 MT LCE/year at a fixed-price floor mechanism
  - Risk: Political risk (Milei government's deregulation policies — potential labour disputes)
  - Argentina Risk Rating: MEDIUM (Score: 4.9/10)

5.2 Chile (Atacama Desert — Antofagasta Region)
  - Production capacity: 200,000 MT LCE/year (SQM + Albemarle); largest global reserve base
  - India engagement: KABIL-Chile discussions at exploratory stage; no formal MOU as of Q1 2025
  - Complication: Chile's National Lithium Strategy (announced 2023) favours state control via CODELCO
  - Recommended action: Accelerate G2G negotiations; leverage India-Chile FTA discussions
  - Chile Risk Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH (Score: 5.8/10)

5.3 Bolivia
  - Vast reserves (21 million MT, largest in world) but underdeveloped production
  - YLB (Yacimientos de Litio Bolivianos) partnership with CITIC (China) and CATL underway
  - India opportunity limited unless strategic state-level partnership formed
  - Bolivia Risk Rating: HIGH due to political instability (Score: 7.1/10)

SECTION 6: DOMESTIC LITHIUM RESOURCE STATUS

6.1 Degana-Nagaur Deposit, Rajasthan (GSI Confirmed, 2025)
  - Inferred resource: 5.9 million MT lithium ore at 0.28% Li₂O grade
  - Lithium carbonate equivalent: ~380,000 MT LCE (preliminary estimate, subject to further drilling)
  - Current stage: G3 exploration completed; G2 drilling programme initiated Jan 2025
  - Earliest production: FY2031-32 (optimistic scenario)
  - Processing challenge: Spodumene-type mineralogy requires hydrometallurgical processing 
    (no existing refinery in India; NALCO/MECL evaluating greenfield processing plant at Jodhpur)

6.2 Reasi, Jammu & Kashmir (GSI Survey 2023)
  - Original 2023 estimate: 5.9 million tonnes of lithium ore (widely reported; now revised)
  - Revised assessment (GSI, Q4 2024): 1.2 million MT Li ore at 0.18% Li₂O
  - LCE: ~39,600 MT — materially lower than original reports
  - Status: Under MECL detailed drilling; environmental clearance pending

SECTION 8: RECOMMENDATIONS

PRIORITY 1: Initiate spot procurement of 15,000 MT LCE immediately (Q1 FY2025-26) at current 
            depressed prices — estimated cost INR 1,240 Crore. This would push coverage to 
            13.5 months, significantly reducing vulnerability.

PRIORITY 2: Fast-track formal G2G agreement with Chile through MoM-MEA joint working group; 
            target MOU signing by September 2025.

PRIORITY 3: Commission NALCO/MECL feasibility study for domestic lithium processing refinery 
            (Rajasthan or Gujarat) — INR 4,500 Crore capex estimate (MECON preliminary study).

PRIORITY 4: Negotiate KABIL-SQM Chile long-term supply contract (minimum 20,000 MT LCE/year 
            from FY2026-27 to FY2035-36) with price capped at USD 14,000/MT LCE.

PRIORITY 5: Establish lithium futures hedging programme via MCX/SEBI — requires regulatory 
            amendment; propose inclusion in Union Budget FY2025-26 Finance Bill.
